Multi-Stimuli-Responsive Smart Materials for Soft Robotics

Objective

Hydrogels are porous hydrophilic polymer networks that exhibit properties of both solids and liquids. They are multi-stimuli-responsive, bio-compatible, and broadly applicable in the field of soft robotics.
To address the lack of modelling dedicated for any specific application of the material, I built a multi-physics simulation model, coupled with experimental validation, for the actuation response of NaAc/AAm hydrogel beams in a NaCl solution under an electric field.

Experimental Methodology

I established a repeatable experimental system for fabricating hydrogel beams and conducting deformation tests to validate the corresponding simulation.

Simulational Methodology

I constructed the simulation around four main governing mechanisms into the system: diffusion-convection and Poisson’s equation for the ionic species and electric potential; continuity and momentum equation for the fluid pressure and gel displacement. I discretized the system using node-based finite-difference method and jointly used different non-linear solvers from Python and MATLAB for the iterative algorithm.

Experimental Results

The experimental results include the swelling behaviour of NaAc-AAm hydrogels in a NaCl solution, along with its bending in a NaCl solution under an electric field. The data demonstrates how the deformation is affected by parameters such as voltage, crosslinker concentration and salt bath concentration, which allows a way to verify the validity of the numerical scheme.

Simulational Results

The simulational results comprise the prediction of the deformation of a generic electro-active hydrogel and how it is affected by voltage, crosslinker density and salt bath concentration. It also offers insights into the distribution of the all the ionic concentrations and the electric potential across the entire domain. The validity of the results is evaluated from various perspectives, including but not limited to comparing it against the experimental results.

Main limitation

  • To avoid electrolysis and ensure visible bending, only a limited range of voltage and electrode distance can be used.
  • Due to the intrinsic accuracy limitation of the selected local nonlinear solvers, the simulation was only able to produce consistent results when the voltage varies from 0.0625V to 0.1875V, the salt concentration varies from 8.75mol/m3 to 11.25 mol/m3 and the reference water volume percentage varies from 0.6 to 0.9.
  • There is a mismatch of used parameter ranges between the experiment and simulation, which partially reduces the credibility of experimental validation.

Future Work

  • Deterministic global non-linear solvers and higher computational power will be used to improve the numerical accuracy and consistency of the simulation.
  • To address the parameter mismatch, a set of usable parameter ranges would be first investigated computationally. Usable parameter combinations will be tested out to match the simulation.
